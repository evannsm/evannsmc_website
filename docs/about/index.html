About Me

A Bit of Backstory.

I grew up in Puerto Rico, finished high school and got my Bachelors in electrical engineering with a focus on autonomous systems in Texas, and now spend my days at Georgia Tech working on safe autonomy for quadrotors. I spend a lot of time thinking about rigorous control theory, hardware deployment and, most importantly, community building, which is why BORI means just as much to me as the research I work on.

Where I'm From

Puerto Rico

I was born and raised on the island until I was 9. Puerto Rico shaped my values, my drive, and my sense of community. It is a big part of why I co-founded BORI (Boricuas Organized for Impact) at Georgia Tech and why staying connected to Puerto Rican culture still matters to me.

Community

BORI

I helped co-found BORI (Boricuas Organized for Impact) to build community and celebrate Puerto Rican culture at Georgia Tech. It has become one of the main ways I stay connected to home while also making campus feel more welcoming for other Puerto Rican students.

  • We create spaces that feel culturally familiar, from kickoff events to game nights and shared Puerto Rican food.
  • We try to be useful, not just social, helping newer students find people, resources, and a sense of belonging.
  • The organization reflects a part of me that matters just as much as the research: building community that lasts.
