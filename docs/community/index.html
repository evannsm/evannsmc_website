Community

BORI at Georgia Tech

I co-founded BORI (Boricuas Organized for Impact) to make sure Puerto Rican students at Georgia Tech had a space that felt familiar, welcoming, and genuinely useful. The goal was never just to start another campus organization. It was to build something that could help people feel recognized and at home.

  • We built events around culture, food, and friendship rather than treating community as an afterthought.
  • We focused on making newer students feel less alone when arriving in Atlanta and Georgia Tech.
  • BORI gave me a way to stay connected to Puerto Rico while helping make campus feel more human for others too.

That side of my life matters for the same reason the research does: both are about building systems that people can trust.

Outside the Lab

What keeps me grounded

A lot of the non-research side of my life still comes back to culture, creativity, and being with people. That usually means staying active, cooking, dancing, reading, writing, and making time for spaces that feel alive rather than purely technical.

Fitness

Staying active helps me reset, keep some balance, and make sure life does not collapse entirely into code, papers, and lab work.

Cooking

Food is one of the easiest ways for me to bring people together, especially when I can cook something that feels tied to home.

Dance

Dance keeps me expressive and connected to culture rather than letting everything collapse into work and theory.

Reading & Writing

That is where I usually go when I want to slow down, think clearly, and come back to ideas in a less technical way.
